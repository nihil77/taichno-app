8 Foundational Taijiquan
+

Taijiquan, often referred to as Tai Chi, is a traditional Chinese martial art that has evolved into a popular practice for physical and mental well-being.

Horse Stance (Ma Bu)
Description: This fundamental pose is a cornerstone of Taijiquan, emphasizing a balanced, rooted posture. It is key to the practice, fostering stability and strength.

Bow-Arrow Stance (Gong JianBu)
Description: This foundational posture embodies the balance of a warrior and the fluidity of a dancer. It focuses on alignment and inner strength, providing a step-by-step guide for practitioners.

Sitting on Crossed Legs Stance (Zuo Pan Bu)
Description: Combining principles of stability and inner peace, this foundational posture is an essential aspect of Taijiquan. The practice encourages tranquility, emphasizing posture and breath.

Four-Six Stance (Si Liu Bu)
Description: This unique posture captures the delicate balance between strength and flexibility. Exploring the intricacies of the Four-Six Stance refines understanding and execution.

Tame the Tiger Stance (Fu Hu Bu)
Description: Mastering this foundational technique involves a profound connection between mind and body. Real-time guidance and personalized feedback enhance stance, balance, and the overall Taijiquan practice.

False Stance (Xuan Ji Bu or Xu Bu)
Description: This foundational technique challenges balance and precision. The platform guides practitioners through weight distribution and alignment in real-time learning experiences.

Golden Rooster Standing on One Leg Stance (Jin Gi Du Li)
Description: This intricate technique demands concentration, balance, and stability. Real-time guidance and interactive learning experiences assist in mastering this challenging stance.

Squat Stance (Zuo Dun)
Description: As a cornerstone for Taijiquan practice, the Squat Stance emphasizes strength, balance, and flexibility. Interactive sessions offer comprehensive guidance to enhance understanding.
